A comprehensive redesign of the Student Profile



Role
Lead Product Designer
Project Duration
4 weeks
Tools
Figma, Miro, Mixpanel

* * * * *

Overview


Problem

The current state of the Student Profile falls short in prioritizing information effectively and guiding users toward crucial actions. The existing design lacks a structured hierarchy for presenting data, coupled with an inflexible layout that challenges the visibility of all panels. Additionally, certain panels have remained untouched, contributing to outdated functionality that hinders the user experience.


Caption here



Solution

Paired with a product manager, I led a comprehensive redesign that addressed these issues, providing a clear information hierarchy, guiding users to key actions—all while telling a story about a student's progress in each content area.


Jump to Final Designs

Context

The Portal serves as a school management tool. The Student Profile page is crucial for many Portal users, offering a granular view of a specific student's data across various content areas, including attendance, academics, and credits progress. According to product analytics, the Student Profile is the most visited page in the Portal.

There was a UI redesign of the entire Portal right before this project which improved the look and feel of the profile. However, the Student Profile page still felt dense with information and the overall user experience of the page could be improved upon.



Caption here



The recent UI redesign of the Portal provided an opportunity for our team to implement a comprehensive redesign of the Student Profile, covering diverse school types (including elementary schools, middle schools, and high schools) across New York City.




* * * * *

Discovery

Kickoff

At the start of the project, there were many different design possibilites to explore. Although I already knew some of major pain points in the student profile through existing user and stakeholder feedback, there was no defined mission or specific goals for the ideal student profile experience. To address this, I organized concurrent brainstorming sessions and exploratory interviews with the product team, leveraging their existing knowledge of the student profile within tight time constraints.


Defining Success

I facilitated a brainstorming session with the members of the product team, including the Director of Product Management, and the Head of Design to understand what success looks like and create long-term and short-term goals for the student profile design, as well as identify key deliverables.




Understanding Workflows

The student profile has 12 panels, each addressing key content areas. As these panels were created individually by different teams over time, there was siloed knowledge about their design. To address this, I conducted exploratory interviews with product managers from various verticals to understand user needs and common workflows for each panel.



In interviews, I discussed stakeholder requests, such as reducing the height of the student profile header, since many of our users access the Portal on smaller screens like Chromebooks in schools. Some stakeholders even suggested removing metrics from the header.


However, I learned that the purpose of these metrics is to provide valuable contextual information to users across different panels. Product managers also acknowledged that some metrics may be redundant or unnecessary, requiring further consideration. Notably, the current header lacks the year-to-date (YTD) attendance rate, which is crucial for understanding a student's performance across all panels. For example, if a user sees a student struggling in the Current Programs panel, knowing their YTD attendance rate adds context to the situation, revealing issues like chronic absenteeism.


* * * * *

Approach


At this point, my product manager and I decided to split the design work into phases. This first phase would tackle redesigning the overall layout of the profile, leaving the individual panels for the second phase. The main pain points addressed in Phase 1 were the information overload, difficulty in understanding student status, inefficient information retrieval, and the need for humanizing the data. The goal for this phase is to create a design with a clear information hierarchy, strategic nudges for key actions, and a humanized narrative about the student.

My initial gameplan consisted of:

  • Exploring a new layout for the student profile
  • Introducing navigation so users can see at a glance all the panels available and get to those panels easily
  • Rethinking the student profile header while accomodating stakeholders' request to reduce the height student profile header

Exploring a New Layout

I designed three blockframe options for the student profile layout, prioritizing navigation space. Addressing the user pain point of overwhelming data, I also explored adding a section for 'action items' to highlight actionable next steps.

Although the third option was favored, there was a suggestion to consider future mobile responsiveness. To make mobile responsiveness on the page easiler to implement in the future, I finalized the design with a two-column layout and integrated action items into the navigation using notification badges.

New Way to Navigate

Utilizing product analytics and insights from the product team, I designed a left-hand sidenav for the student profile. The order of panels reflects common user workflows and click rates. To enhance visual appeal and reduce text density, I incorporated icons into the navigation.




Moving the History Log

To accommodate the new navigation bar and preserve a two-column layout, I relocated the history log from the right side of the student profile to a new panel. Despite some pushback from the product team, my manager and I decided to proceed and gather user feedback during testing. Concurrently, another team was implementing a task management system for the Portal. To integrate tasks within a student's profile, I collaborated with their designer to create a version of the history log panel with added tasks.




Revamping the Header

The header needed a height reduction, so I created several mockups where the information in the header was condensed to two lines. Likewise, the Homepage redesign team decided to add friendly imagery in the Homepage header, which inspired us to add visuals to the Student Profile header as well. Since I have been spearheading the illustration work throughout the Portal, I illustrated the final header for both pages.


* * * * *

User Insights


I created a prototype showcasing the new Student Profile designs and conducted brief usability testing.

Insights Include:

  • Participants highlighted the significance of scrolling between panels, particularly for tasks like adding a graduation plan. Maintaining this scrolling capability was deemed essential.
  • I received a lot of feedback from participants about the information available in the student header, which helped define with metrics were crucial.
  • Surprisingly, participants found the new history log/notes panel more discoverable, with some perceiving it as a new and exciting feature. This positive response confirmed the validity of relocating the history log to a new panel.

Final Considerations


Student Snapshot

While addressing goals like improving information hierarchy and discoverability in the student profile redesign, a key focus was creating a narrative for the student and enhancing quick understanding for users. Based on insights from product manager interviews, I designed a new student snapshot panel to live on the top of the page.




This design prioritizes crucial information, particularly outstanding items, prompting users to take necessary actions. After multiple iterations and stakeholder feedback, we finalized a design that presents high-level student information, encouraging users to delve deeper if needed by clicking into any of the containers, bringing users straight to that panel to learn more.




Thinking Through User Flows

With the introduction of the navigation sidebar, I needed to think through the interaction between the sidebar and the existing collapsing and expanding experience of the panels.



Further, a common workflow for users to navigate between user profiles to complete tasks for multiple students, one by one. At this point, there were already inconsistencies in the collapsed and expand state in the panels when going from one student profile to the other. There were also inconsistencies on the default panel visible when the user flipped through student profiles. I designed diagrams these flows, collaborating with engineers to implement these experiences in the backend.

Takeaways


It's still the early days of the launch of this redesign, but we've received overwhelmingly positive feedback from users.

" Thinking about insurance just stresses me out but I want to learn what it all means one day. "
" I'm okay with a completely online insurance company--it's not like I'm going to go in person anywhere during COVID."


In four weeks, we undertook a significant overhaul of the Student Profile in the Portal to address usability challenges systematically. Handling a tight deadline and numerous redesign possibilities, I realized how crucial it is to take a phased approach when tackling a project this large. Collaborating closely with stakeholders and seamlessly incorporating features from other teams made it a collective effort. The revamped Student Profile isn't just updated; it's now user-centric, polished, and adaptable.
